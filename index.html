About Me

Bridging Academic Pedagogy & Cutting-edge Research

I serve as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at Techno College of Engineering Agartala (TCEA). Concurrently, I am pursuing my Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in Computer Science and Engineering at the National Institute of Technology Agartala.

My research group works extensively on advanced pattern extraction models, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Generative Adversarial Networks (specifically Classifier Posterior GANs), and Vision Transformers. Our focus lies in solving predictive healthcare diagnostics, underwater visibility mapping, and video compression systems.

As a faculty member, I have had the privilege of teaching core computer science courses across multiple institutions. I am deeply committed to mentoring student projects, organizing hackathons (such as Impactech 2K24), and organizing faculty development programs.

Research Focus & Laboratories

Targeting pattern recognition, deep fake security mechanisms, and automated bio-medical classification systems.

Deepfake Creation & Detection

Researching Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and forensic systems to isolate, verify, and detect manipulated media in digital channels.

Medical Imaging Diagnostics

Applying Vision Transformers and Convolutional neural nets to identify anomalies in lung CT scans and automated malaria screenings.

Multimodal Sentiment Analysis

Fusing audio features with BERT lyrical encoders to evaluate speaker moods, sentiment variations, and music genres automatically.

Smart Industrial IoT

Integrating sensors and edge computing models for water resource conservation, smart irrigation systems, and elderly home health monitoring.
